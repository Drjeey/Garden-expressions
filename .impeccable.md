## Design Context

### Users
Homeowners and décor enthusiasts in East Africa (Uganda, Kenya) browsing during leisure time, likely on mobile. They are taste-led: they want to feel desire and trust before they act. They are not in a hurry — they are shopping aspirationally, the way you'd page through a beautifully shot home magazine. Price is secondary to perceived quality and fit.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Grown, curated, grounded.

Not glamorous in a loud way — earned, like a garden that took years to cultivate. The brand should feel like something real, rooted in a specific place (East Africa), with genuine craft behind it. Anti-reference: tech startup green, generic Shopify botanical template, Pinterest mood-board aesthetics.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Nature-forward, organic, earthy.** Think raw textures, aged materials, the patina of terracotta left outside, the weight of a stone planter. Design that looks like it grew rather than was assembled. The current dark forest green + gold palette already has bones — the gap is in texture, tactility, and compositional confidence. Light and warmth should come from within (gold, cream) rather than from literal brightness. Dark mode is correct for the audience context (evening browsing, warm lighting indoors).

### Design Principles
1. **Tactility over gloss** — surfaces should suggest material weight: clay, moss, bark, linen — not glass or plastic.
2. **Trust before conversion** — every page section should earn confidence, not rush to a CTA.
3. **East African specificity** — the site should feel located. References to Kampala, Nairobi, UGX pricing, and the equatorial plant palette are features, not footnotes.
4. **Restraint in motion** — animations should feel like growth, not tech interactivity. Slow reveals, no bounce.
5. **Hierarchy over variety** — fewer, better-differentiated typographic levels; less decorative noise, more compositional clarity.
